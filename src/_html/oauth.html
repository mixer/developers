Beam OAuth

Quick details:

(PS: Looking for the API endpoints? Click here)

  • Authorize endpoint: https://beam.pro/oauth/authorize
  • Token endpoint: https://beam.pro/api/v1/oauth/token

Introduction

If you want to act as another user in chat, access a users' private resources as part of your app, or just want to use Beam credentials to authenticate your users, it's best to use OAuth so the users' credentials don't need to be stored on your servers.

OAuth is a system that replaces traditional user/password combos with app-specific tokens. Your app can request a set of tokens for a certain user with a specific set of permissions. This way your app only gets access to what it needs and all other details will stay hidden. It's a win-win scenario for both your app's functionality, and our user's privacy.

To start using OAuth create an application as described below.

Registering your application

To create an application head over to the Manage OAuth page! (You may need to authorize the developers site first). Once there, click the Create New button in the top right corner and it'll open the creation form.

On this page you'll need to enter some basic details about your application, like its name, website and logo. All of these details will be displayed publically to users of your app.

Hosts

The hosts parameter is an important one; it tells us what domains your application can redirect to. This should be set to domains you control. You can use wildcards, for example: *.beam.pro will allow redirects to all subdomains on beam.pro, but not beam.pro itself. The list of domains is comma separated if you want to add multiple ones.

Using OAuth

The OAuth 2.0 protocol is simple enough, but that doesn't mean you should implement it from scratch. Most popular languages will have a library to easily interface with an OAuth server.

If you're interested in reading an in depth explanation of how OAuth works head here.

To use our OAuth implementation you'll just need a few details which can be found at the top of the page and on your Manage OAuth page.

OAuth Scopes

To request access to a users' account you need to use scopes. Scopes limit the amount of access a certain application has to the account. Below are all the scopes you can use. If you're requesting multiple, make sure to use spaces in between them!

  • user:details:self View your email address and other private details. (Handy for authenticating the user without needing edit access)
  • user:analytics:self View your user analytics
  • user:delete:self Delete your account.
  • user:log:self View your user security log.
  • user:twofactor:self Update your two-factor settings.
  • user:update:self Update your account, including your email and password.
  • achievement:view:self View your earned achievements.
  • channel:analytics:self View your channel analytics.
  • channel:details:self View your channel details, including stream key.
  • channel:follow:self Follow and unfollow other channels.
  • channel:partnership:self Manage your partnership status.
  • channel:update:self Update your channel settings, including stream key.
  • chat:bypass_links Bypass links being disallowed in chat. (Only mods/streamer in their own chat)
  • chat:bypass_slowchat Bypass slowchat settings on channels. (Only mods/streamer in their own chat)
  • chat:chat Interact with chats on your behalf.
  • chat:clear_messages Clear messages in chats where authorized.
  • chat:connect Connect to chat.
  • chat:edit_options Edit chat options, including links settings and slowchat.
  • chat:giveaway_start Start a giveaway in chats where authorized.
  • chat:poll_start Start a poll in chats where authorized.
  • chat:poll_vote Vote in chat polls.
  • chat:remove_message Remove own and other's messages in chat. (Only mods/streamer in their own chat)
  • log:view:self View and manage the your security log.
  • notification:update:self Create and manage your notifications.
  • notification:view:self View your notifications.
  • oauth:manage:self View and manage your OAuth clients.
  • oauth:authorize:self Authorize other applications to access your account.
  • resource:delete:self Remove your access to emoticons and other graphical resources.
  • resource:find:self View emoticons and other graphical resources you have access to.
  • subscription:view:self View who you're subscribed to.
  • transaction:view:self View your pending transactions.
